# Design System Specification: Dark Mode Technical Edition

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: The Digital Foreman

This design system is anchored by the **"Digital Foreman"** creative north star. It moves beyond the generic "dark mode" aesthetic to create a high-end, editorial environment that feels like a precision-engineered workspace. 

The aesthetic is characterized by **Industrial Brutalism refined through Technical Luxury.** We avoid standard templates by utilizing intentional asymmetry, extreme typographic scale shifts, and a depth model based on material density rather than artificial lighting. The interface should feel as sturdy as a steel beam but as precise as a laser level.

## 2. Colors & Surface Logic

The palette is a high-contrast blend of deep carbon neutrals and "Safety Orange" accents. We define space through tonal shifts, never through decorative lines.

### The Palette (Material Design Convention)
*   **Primary (Safety Orange):** `#ffb599` (Base) / `#ff5f00` (Container)
*   **Background / Surface:** `#131313` (The void)
*   **Surface Tiers:** 
    *   `surface_container_lowest`: `#0e0e0e` (Inset depth)
    *   `surface_container_low`: `#1c1b1b` (Standard sectioning)
    *   `surface_container_high`: `#2a2a2a` (Elevated panels)
*   **On-Surface (Typography):** `#e5e2e1` (High-contrast silver)

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Explicit Instruction:** Do not use 1px solid borders for sectioning or containment. Traditional borders feel "cheap" and "templated." 
*   **Definition through Shift:** Separate a sidebar from a main feed by moving from `surface` (#131313) to `surface_container_low` (#1c1b1b).
*   **Definition through Space:** Use the Spacing Scale (e.g., `24` / 5.5rem) to create structural chasms between content blocks.

### The Glass & Gradient Rule
For floating utility panels or "Heads-Up Displays" (HUDs), use `surface_container_highest` at 70% opacity with a `20px` backdrop-blur. To give the Safety Orange "soul," use a linear gradient for primary CTAs: `primary_container` (#ff5f00) to `primary` (#ffb599) at a 135-degree angle.

## 3. Typography: Space Grotesk

We use **Space Grotesk** exclusively. Its geometric, monospaced-leaning construction reinforces the technical "Digital Foreman" identity.

*   **Display (lg/md):** Used for "Hero" technical specs or section headers. Use `display-lg` (3.5rem) with `-0.02em` letter spacing to feel massive and authoritative.
*   **Headline (sm/md):** The workhorse for editorial layouts. Headlines should be set in Semi-Bold to ensure they "cut" through the dark background.
*   **Body (md/lg):** Set at `body-md` (0.875rem) for standard technical documentation. Ensure a line height of at least 1.5 to prevent "shimmering" on dark OLED screens.
*   **Labels (sm/md):** Used for metadata and status tags. These should always be uppercase with `+0.05em` tracking to mimic industrial stamping.

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering

In the "Digital Foreman" world, depth is about physical stacking of materials, not floating in a vacuum.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Place a `surface_container_lowest` (#0e0e0e) card inside a `surface_container_high` (#2a2a2a) section to create a "carved out" effect. This mimics a tool tray or an engraved industrial plate.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** Avoid black shadows. If a floating element (like a Modal) requires lift, use a shadow color derived from `on_surface` (#e5e2e1) at 4% opacity with a `40px` blur. It should look like a soft glow of light hitting a dark surface.
*   **The Ghost Border Fallback:** If accessibility requires a stroke (e.g., input fields), use `outline_variant` (#5b4137) at 20% opacity. It must be felt, not seen.

## 5. Components

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** A solid block of `primary_container` (#ff5f00). No border. Corner radius `sm` (0.125rem) for a sharp, machined look.
*   **Tertiary:** Text-only in `primary` (#ffb599) with a `label-md` uppercase style. 

### Cards & Lists
*   **Rule:** Forbid divider lines.
*   **Execution:** Separate list items using a `surface_container` shift on hover, or use `1.5` (0.3rem) vertical gaps from the spacing scale to reveal the background color beneath.

### Input Fields
*   **Style:** Underline only or "Carved" (`surface_container_lowest`). Use a 2px `primary` accent bar on the left side during the `focus` state to mimic a cursor or a measuring tool.

### Technical HUD (New Component)
*   A fixed-position container for critical data. Uses `surface_bright` at 80% opacity, `xl` (0.75rem) roundedness, and a `secondary` (#c8c6c6) label-sm typography.

## 6. Do’s and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Use Intentional Asymmetry:** Align text to a 12-column grid but leave the last 4 columns empty in hero sections to create editorial "breathing room."
*   **Embrace High Contrast:** Keep technical data in `on_surface` (silver) and only use Safety Orange for critical interaction points.
*   **Use the Spacing Scale:** Stick strictly to the defined increments (e.g., `8`, `12`, `16`) to maintain a "rhythmic" industrial layout.

### Don't:
*   **Don't use 100% Black:** Pure black (#000000) feels "dead." Use our `surface` (#131313) to allow for depth and shadows.
*   **Don't use Rounded Corners for everything:** Keep `DEFAULT` at `0.25rem`. Large rounded corners (pills) feel too "consumer/soft" for a technical foreman's tool.
*   **Don't use standard Dividers:** If you feel the need to draw a line, use a background color change instead.
